Lecture 1 — Biochemical Bases of Behavior · Introduction
🔬 Second Messenger Signaling — Master Map
⚡ cAMP → PKA → CREB Pathway
🌊 Calcium Signaling Cascade
Key Concepts — Lecture 1
📌 cAMP / PKA
cAMP Signaling in the Brain
Gs-coupled receptor → activates Adenylyl Cyclase → ATP → cAMP.
cAMP binds PKA R-subunits (cooperative) → releases active C-subunits.
Targets: Ion channels, CREB (→ gene expression), AMPA receptors.
Brain regions: Hippocampus (memory via CREB), Striatum (reward/motor), Amygdala (emotional memory).
PKA degraded by PDEs (phosphodiesterases — 11 subtypes, 100+ isoforms).
Drug link: PDE4 inhibitor rolipram ↑ cAMP → antidepressant effects.
🌊 Calcium
Ca²⁺ as Intracellular Messenger
Resting Ca²⁺ ~100 nM; signaling raises it to >10 µM near membrane.
Sources: NMDA-Rs, VOCCs (CaV1/2/3), IP3R (ER), Ryanodine Rs.
Sensors: Calmodulin (EF-hand) → CaMKII → LTP. Synaptotagmin (C2 domain) → NT vesicle fusion.
Buffers: Parvalbumin, Calbindin D-28k, ER (calreticulin), Mitochondria (uniporter).
Termination: NCX (low affinity/high capacity), PMCA, SERCA.
💊 Lipid Messengers
IP3 / DAG / PIP3 Pathways
PLC cleaves PIP2 → IP3 + DAG.
IP3 → binds ER IP3R → Ca²⁺ release.
DAG → activates PKC (C1 domain binding).
PIP3 pathway: PI3K phosphorylates PIP2 → PIP3 → recruits Akt (via PH domain) → pro-survival. PTEN = tumor suppressor that dephosphorylates PIP3.
Sphingolipids: Ceramide (apoptosis), Sphingosine-1-P (pro-survival via GPCRs).
⚡ Second Messengers
The 4 Classes at a Glance
1. Cyclic nucleotides — cAMP, cGMP (cytosolic)
2. Lipids — DAG, IP3, PIP3 (membrane)
3. Ions — Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺
4. Gases/radicals — NO, CO
cGMP targets PKG (dimeric, R+C in same chain). PKG mainly in brain/lungs/vascular. Sildenafil (Viagra) blocks PDE5 → ↑ cGMP → smooth muscle relaxation.
Eicosanoids: Prostaglandins (COX pathway), Leukotrienes (LOX) from arachidonic acid. Aspirin inhibits COX.
Lecture 2 — Molecular Aspects of Mood & Anxiety
🧬 Neurotransmitter Imbalances in Depression
🔁 HPA Axis Dysregulation in Depression
Key Concepts — Lecture 2
🧠 BDNF
BDNF in Depression
Decreased in hippocampus and PFC. Binds TrkB receptor → activates 3 cascades: MAPK/ERK (survival + CREB), PI3K/Akt (anti-apoptotic), PLCγ (Ca²⁺ + synaptic strength).
Bcl-2 is major neuroprotective protein downstream. Antidepressants upregulate cAMP → CREB → BDNF expression. Mice lacking TrkB don't respond to antidepressants.
🔁 Wnt Pathway
Wnt/β-Catenin in Mood
Wnt binds Frizzled + LRP5/6 → activates Dishevelled → recruits Axin away from destruction complex → β-catenin accumulates → enters nucleus → TCF/LEF → gene expression (c-Myc, Cyclin D1).
Without Wnt: APC + Axin + GSK-3β + CK1 complex phosphorylates β-catenin → ubiquitination → degradation.
GSK-3β inhibition (e.g., by lithium) → accumulates β-catenin → hippocampal neurogenesis.
💊 GSK-3
GSK-3 & Lithium
GSK-3β phosphorylates MAP-1B → destabilizes microtubules → regulates axon growth. Most signals inactivate GSK-3 (insulin, NGF, BDNF via PI3K).
Lithium inhibits GSK-3β (therapeutic) + inositol monophosphatase (inositol depletion hypothesis). Also attenuates PKC α and ε in frontal cortex/hippocampus. G-protein (Gαs) levels ↑ in bipolar postmortem brain.
📊 Bipolar
Bipolar I vs II
BP-I: ≥1 manic episode (≥7 days, may require hospitalization, psychosis 50%). BP-II: hypomania (≥4 days) + ≥1 MDD — no full mania. Both: mixed episodes, suicidality.
Lifetime US prevalence: 20.8% mood disorders (16.6% MDD + 3.9% BD). Women higher rates. Recurrent in ~80%. HPA axis hyperactive in both mania and depression. Thyroid: BD shows abnormal HPT axis, T3 high in mania.
Lecture 3 — Molecular Bases of Main Signaling Pathways in Neocortex
🧩 Neocortex: Excitatory vs Inhibitory Balance
🔋 Adenylyl Cyclase Isoforms in Brain
Key Concepts — Lecture 3
⚡ PKA Targets
PKA in Neocortex
PKA = heterotetramer (2R + 2C subunits). cAMP binds R cooperatively → releases C. Phosphorylates 300–500 proteins. AKAPs anchor PKA near substrates (10–15 per cell).
PKA phosphorylates AMPA receptors, CREB, cytoskeletal proteins, ion channels, AC5/AC6 (negative feedback). Epac = cAMP-GEF → Rap1/Rap2 → MAP kinase. CNG channels = directly gated by cAMP (olfaction, pacemaker HCN). Caffeine/theophylline block PDEs → ↑ cAMP.
🧩 PLC/PKC
PLC → PKC Cascade
PLC cleaves PIP2 → IP3 + DAG. 13 mammalian PLC isozymes. PLCβ (GPCRs via Gq) and PLCγ (RTKs, phosphorylated) are main brain isoforms.
PKC families: Conventional (need Ca²⁺ + DAG), Novel (DAG only, 100x higher affinity), Atypical (no Ca²⁺/DAG, protein scaffolds). PKC translocation to membrane = activation hallmark. PKC phosphorylates EGF/insulin receptors → internalization (negative feedback). Li⁺ blocks inositol monophosphatase (inositol depletion hypothesis).
🧬 CaMKII
CaMKII & Synaptic Plasticity
Highly abundant in cortex. Ca²⁺ + calmodulin → CaMKII activation → modifies synaptic proteins → LTP. Also regulates dendritic spine actin via Rho GTPases.
CaMKII inhibits AC3. CaMKIV inhibits AC1 (negative counterbalance). CaMKIV KO → anxiolytic phenotype. AC1 critical for synaptic plasticity/LTP in hippocampus/cortex.
💊 Antidepressants
cAMP & Antidepressant Action
Antidepressants ↑ 5-HT/NE/DA → activate GPCRs → ↑ cAMP → ↑ PKA → ↑ CREB → ↑ BDNF. Rolipram (PDE4 inhibitor) also ↑ cAMP → antidepressant but causes nausea.
PDE4 highly expressed in hippocampus and limbic regions. Upregulation of CREB in transgenic mice → antidepressant response independent of drug. Ongoing search for selective PDE4 isozyme inhibitors with fewer side effects.
Lecture 4 — Neurochemistry of Sexual Behavior & Communication
❤️ Three Stages of Love — Neurochemical Map
🗣 FOXP2 — Speech & Language Gene
Key Concepts — Lecture 4
❤️ Chemistry of Love
The Love Molecules
PEA → NE + DA (lust trigger). DA/NE = elation, euphoria, sleeplessness. 5-HT ↓ = obsessive thoughts. Oxytocin = bonding glue (touching → release).
Vasopressin: post-sex, monogamy, ties feelings to memories. NGF high first year of love → returns to baseline after year 1. Endorphins (opiates) → phase 3 calm/attachment. Blocking oxytocin + DA receptors → loss of motherly instinct. Blocking vasopressin → promotes infidelity.
🧠 HPG Axis
Hypothalamus-Pituitary-Gonadal Axis
Hypothalamus → GnRH → Anterior pituitary → LH + FSH → Gonads → Testosterone / Estrogen / Progesterone.
5 lover types: DA-dominant (novelty-seeking), 5-HT-dominant (calm/conventional), Testosterone-dominant (logical/competitive), Estrogen-dominant (intuitive/verbal), Oxytocin/Vasopressin (monogamous/family). Romantic love = elevation of DA/NE + suppression of 5-HT.
🗣 Lateralization
Brain Lateralization & Language
DA release is left-lateralized in striatum during speech. Low DA → Parkinson's speech decline. ASD: right temporal cortex higher GABA vs neurotypical.
Left dominant sensorimotor cortex has higher GABA. Acetylcholine triggers larynx/tongue/lip muscles for articulation. Adrenaline + cortisol (public speaking fear) → ↑ HR, sweating, shortness of breath. GABA overall hemispherically symmetric but locally specific.
🧬 FOXP
FOXP2 & Speech Gene Family
4 members: Foxp1, Foxp2, Foxp3, Foxp4. Only Foxp1/2/4 require dimerization. FOXP2 mutation → verbal dyspraxia. Ortholog in many species; Neanderthals carried human-identical FOXP2.
Zinc finger + leucine zipper enable dimerization. PolyGln tracts in Foxp1/2 (not 4) → protein-protein interaction. Forkhead domain: ~80% identical within Foxp subfamily. Among 5% most conserved mammalian proteins. Humans differ from mice by only 3 amino acids.
